Executive Summary

The dependency scan shows a moderate security exposure: four high-severity CVEs and ten medium-severity issues across 15 packages, with several high-impact fixes available for urllib3 and black that should be prioritized. Upgrading everything available yields the greatest reduction in exposure but carries a substantial probable breakage risk driven mainly by major-version bumps to pytest and pip; that path should only be attempted with comprehensive CI, canary releases, and staged rollouts. A balanced remediation path is the recommended near-term approach because it removes most medium/high CVEs while keeping probable breakage low to moderate. That path reduces exposure significantly with fewer disruptive upgrades, leaving the pip CVE that currently has no upstream fix as an outstanding issue. The most important remediation outcome is therefore twofold: apply the balanced upgrades now to remediate the bulk of vulnerabilities, and implement immediate runtime mitigations for the pip GHSA-58qw-9mgm-455v issue by restricting runtime pip operations, isolating build and packaging environments, and replacing automated pip usage where feasible. Also plan a separate, well-tested change window for higher-risk major upgrades such as pytest and pip, and close detection gaps (for example, the dynamic import of PyYAML) so future scans reliably surface usage. With these steps—balanced upgrades, targeted mitigations for no-fix CVEs, and staged testing of major bumps—the team can materially lower exposure while managing upgrade risk.
